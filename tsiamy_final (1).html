At first we need to obtain a graphical representation of the continous variable for the various levels of the categorical variable. Besides always being a good start to visualize our data, it can be a first step to check whether the sample distributions are close to normal (one of the assumptions of the ANOVA methodology). Apart from that, it is always beneficial to create a box plot to compare the differences of Y between the levels of W. Even better, we can turn the box plot to a violin plot.
